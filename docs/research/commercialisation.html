MOLO 90-Day UK-First Commercialisation Plan

Strategic commercialization roadmap for moloapp.io
Created: January 2026

Executive Summary

MOLO Overview
MOLO positions itself as the "Chief Household Officer" for busy parents—an AI-powered household management system designed to reduce the mental load through automated task generation, delegation, and eventually autonomous execution. The platform starts with "MOLO Lists" (automated household task organization) and expands toward "MOLO Assist" (AI that can order groceries, buy birthday cards, scan emails) and "MOLO Manager" (comprehensive life admin tooling).

UK-First Strategy Rationale
The UK market offers distinct advantages for parent tech: established NHS-adjacent ecosystems for distribution, strong employer benefits culture, and cultural awareness of the "mental load" concept. UK parents demonstrate willingness to pay for family coordination tools, particularly around school admin and household management.

90-Day Targets Summary

  • Revenue: 100–250 paying UK households
  • Activation Rate: 25–40% (waitlist → activated users)
  • Paid Conversion: 8–15% (activated → paying)
  • Retention: 70%+ D30 for paid users
  • CAC Payback: < 2 months

Key Assumptions

  • MOLO's current waitlist system provides foundation for immediate testing
  • UK parents will adopt subscription model for mental load reduction
  • Partner/spouse adoption is critical for retention
  • Trust-building must precede advanced AI features

Strategic Context

What MOLO Is

Product Positioning: "Chief Household Officer"
MOLO frames itself as the missing executive layer for family operations, handling the invisible work that traditionally falls disproportionately to mothers.

Core Features:

  • MOLO Lists: Automated household task generation tailored to family structure, child ages, and lifestyle
  • MOLO Assist (Coming): AI assistant capable of ordering groceries, purchasing birthday cards, scanning emails for actionable items
  • MOLO Manager (Coming): Life administration including diary management, tracking children's medical records and clothing sizes

Mission & Vision
Mission: Ease the mental load by harnessing technology to give parents practical tools supporting home and professional lives.
Vision: A world where parents (especially mothers) are no longer consumed by mental load and can work toward brighter personal and professional futures.

Current Stage
Pre-scale with active waitlist system. Company registered in 2024, team of 6 employees, currently fundraising.

Week-by-Week Execution Plan

Week 1 — UK Baseline + Instrumentation Setup

Objective: Establish measurement framework and UK-specific messaging foundation.

What to Do:

  • Lock product wedge: "MOLO Lists = Mental Load OS" (no grocery ordering yet)
  • Implement full funnel analytics from landing to paid conversion
  • Create 3 UK-specific landing page variants:
    1. "Stop being the default parent"
    2. "Sunday Reset for families (without spreadsheets)"
    3. "Make the invisible load visible (then share it)"
  • Set up tracking for partner invitation rates (critical UK metric)

Channel Experiments:

  • Landing page A/B tests with UK cultural references
  • Initial organic LinkedIn content (3 posts about decision fatigue)

KPIs:

  • Landing → waitlist: 8–15%
  • Waitlist completion rate: >60%
  • Analytics implementation: 100% funnel coverage

Budget: £300–£600

Build Next: "Mental Load Audit" quiz as lead magnet with UK-specific scoring

Week 2 — UK Messaging Tests + Mental Load Audit Launch

Objective: Find highest-converting message-market fit for UK parents.

What to Do:

  • Launch Mental Load Audit quiz with email capture
  • Create segmented follow-up sequences based on audit results
  • Test 3 core value propositions across channels

Channel Experiments:

  • Meta (UK): 2 audiences × 3 creatives × 2 hooks
    • Audiences: "Parents of young children," "Working parents"
    • Hooks: Default parent / Sunday reset / Invisible load
  • TikTok (UK): 3 UGC-style founder videos
  • LinkedIn: Daily content about mental load and decision fatigue

KPIs:

  • Meta CTR: >1%
  • Landing page conversion: >8%
  • Cost per waitlist signup: £2–£8
  • Audit completion rate: >60%

Budget: £800–£1,500

Build Next: 3-email nurture sequence triggered by audit results

Week 3 — Concierge Beta Launch (UK)

Objective: Generate immediate revenue and gather retention insights.

What to Do:

  • Offer 30 UK households: £129 onboarding + 30 days Family plan
  • Collect baseline metrics: hours/week admin, stress scores, "default parent" identification
  • Conduct live setup calls to understand UK-specific household patterns
  • Document onboarding script for future automation

Channel Experiments:

  • Direct outreach to qualified waitlist members
  • Partner with 2-3 UK parenting coaches for referrals

KPIs:

  • Concierge conversion from contacted waitlist: 3–8%
  • Setup call completion rate: >85%
  • Post-setup NPS: >40
  • Baseline data collection: 100% of participants

Budget: £0–£200 (scheduling tools + admin)

Build Next: Standardized onboarding script + UK household template library

Week 4 — Retention Loop: Sunday Reset Implementation

Objective: Build habit-forming weekly engagement pattern.

What to Do:

  • Develop "Sunday Reset" automated workflow:
    • Auto-generate upcoming week's household plan
    • One-click task reassignment interface
    • Automated reminders (Friday prep + Sunday finalize)
  • Integrate with common UK calendar systems

Channel Experiments:

  • Test SMS vs. email reminder preferences for UK users
  • A/B test reminder timing (Saturday evening vs. Sunday morning)

KPIs:

  • % activated users completing Sunday Reset: >40%
  • D7 retention (activated users): >35%
  • Weekly plan completion rate: >25%

Budget: £0–£300 (email/SMS tooling)

Build Next: Partner invitation UX that feels collaborative, not confrontational

Week 5 — Family Plan Paywall + UK Creator Seeding

Objective: Prove willingness-to-pay beyond concierge model.

What to Do:

  • Launch £14.99/month Family plan with paywall at shared household value
  • Implement annual plan option with discount (£129/year)
  • Launch affiliate program: 15-20% recurring for 12 months

Channel Experiments:

  • Recruit 10 UK micro-creators:
    • "Working mum systems" accounts
    • "ADHD household management" creators
    • "Postpartum/perinatal wellbeing" advocates
  • Provide 90-day free access + tracked referral links
  • Test creator content formats (testimonials vs. tutorials)

KPIs:

  • Activated → Paid conversion: >8%
  • Creator traffic → waitlist CVR: >10%
  • Creator traffic → paid CVR: 1–3%
  • Annual vs. monthly plan selection: track ratio

Budget: £500–£1,500 (creator fees + affiliate platform)

Build Next: UK-specific template pack focused on school admin

Decision Gates & Review Framework

Week 13 Decision Criteria

Scale Consumer Subscription Path If:

  • CAC payback < 2 months consistently
  • D30 retention > 65% for paid users
  • Organic referral rate > 3% monthly
  • Clear path to £1M ARR visible

Pivot to B2B2C Focus If:

  • Employer pilot conversion > 30%
  • B2B ACV > 10x consumer LTV
  • Sales cycle < 6 months
  • Clear channel partner interest

Invest in Couple UX If:

  • Partner adoption rate < 40%
  • Single-user churn > 60% at 30 days
  • Support tickets primarily about "partner won't engage"
  • High NPS from two-adult households vs. single users

Scale vs Pivot Framework

Scaling Indicators:

  • Unit economics healthy and improving
  • Channel saturation not yet reached
  • Product-market fit signals strengthening
  • Team capacity matches growth demands

Pivot Indicators:

  • CAC trends increasing despite optimization
  • Retention declining over time
  • Market feedback suggests different priority
  • Competitive pressure changing landscape

Success Indicators

Leading Indicators (Weeks 1-8):

  • Activation time decreasing
  • Partner invitation acceptance rates
  • Weekly engagement consistency

Lagging Indicators (Weeks 9-13):
